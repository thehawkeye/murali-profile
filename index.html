Dissertation

The Ecosystem Vortex: Understanding Portfolio Diffusion and Lock-in in Platform Ecosystems

Digital platform ecosystems are dynamic entities featuring mediated interactions that generate network effects, modular architectures that enable co-creation, and multi-product portfolios that deeply embed users. This study proposes and validates The Ecosystem Vortex, a socio-technical model integrating architectural and behavioral dynamics explaining lock-in as emerging through three sequential stages: Entry, Engagement, and Entrapment.

Results show that Entry is driven by functional and brand triggers amplified by social influence; Engagement is sustained by ecosystem synergies and network effects; and Entrapment emerges from behavioral inertia and switching costs.

01 — Lock-in is engineered, not emergent

A three-stage process — Entry, Engagement, Entrenchment — each compounding the next.

02 — Ecosystem engagement is the decisive variable

Full mediation (β=0.69): no direct path from adoption to lock-in exists without engagement.

03 — Habit beats switching costs 2.6× to 1

Behavioral inertia (w=0.79) dominates cognitive friction (w=0.30). The moat is automaticity, not architecture.

04 — Social influence is the ignition mechanism

Mimetic desire precedes network externalities — amplifying both adoption and network effects simultaneously.
